A+
September 2012 - June 2013

A+ is an open-source educational platform developed by the research group LeTech (Learning+Technology) of Aalto University School of Science. The abstract idea of A+ is to hide the different technical requirements for different courses and thus reduce the number of educational services that students have to deal with during their studies.

I worked as a research assistant in LeTech as well as the main teaching assistant for the Web Software Development course. My job was to deploy A+ to be used on two courses in Aalto and to develop it further as new requirements emerged and as bugs and weaknesses were discovered.

The use and development of A+ continues by the LeTech group. The code is hosted in GitHub.

 

Scalability and Plug-ins with Django

The main focus of my work with A+ was on the Django back-end. Several scalability issues were found as there has been hundred thousand exercise submissions to the service after the initial launch. I gained deep understanding in ORM while solving those database-related issues.

Another big thing I did, was designing and implementing a plug-in architecture for A+ to allow the research group to build plug-ins related to educational research.

Deployment and Linux

The deployment and maintenance of A+ and separate grading services lead me to work with the Linux command line. For example, I learned to manage linux processes and permissions for secure Apache-based web server which was crucial as we had run student-written programs to grade programming course exercises.

I also wrote some Java code and shell scripts and worked with RabbitMQ and Celery to implement task queuing.

University-grade Research

Most of the people that I worked with at Aalto, were either post-doc researchers or doctoral students. I was honored to work with some of the smartest computer scientists in Finland. I learned a lot about doing serious university grade research.

Jiiri
May 2012 - Present

Jiiri is a time management application with an innovation yet to be seen: Jiiri is able to automatically schedule users' tasks to their calendars as an optimal but realistic execution plan while considering their exsisting calendar events.

Jiiri was developed as a startup company of five university students during the summer of 2012.

 

Founder of a Startup

The idea for Jiiri originated from my own time management problems as a university student. Instead of applying for normal summer jobs, I decided to assemble a team for Jiiri with my like-minded friends to test ourselves as entrepreneurs.

In addition to the technical side, I learned a lot about customer development, sales, creating a business model, leadership and startups in general.

Full Stack with Django and Backbone.js

As the most experienced developer, I was responsible for our software process, architectural design and helping other developers with whatever they got stuck with.

The basis of our technology stack was Django in back-end, Backbone.js in front-end and Tastypie (a REST framework) between them. I worked with the whole stack.

Designing the Scheduling Algorithm

The most challenging and interesting part of my work was designing and implementing the scheduling algorithm. The scheduling algorithm I wrote is unique and I don't want to make it public but I'm willing to show and discuss it if asked, as I am very proud of my work.

Vaihtorinki
September 2012 - January 2013

After the summer 2012, I was hired as a lead developer to a startup company Vaihtorinki. At that point, Vaihtorinki was trying to launch a web service where small companies could temoporarily hire blue-collar workforce from each other.

We used the same tech stack as with Jiiri - Django and Backbone.js. I was responsible for the architectural design and development coordination. In this project, the requirements came straight from the CEO and our developer team of three people implemented whatever she asked.

 

Heavy Javascript Front-end

The Vaihtorinki web app was fully dynamic javascript application. I learned to use advanced javascript technologies such as marionette.js, require.js and grunt.js to handle the complexity of our front-end architecture.

Data Interface

As all the functional and UI code was in the front-end, the back-end had to provide fast and secure data interface with business logic operations. I had to solve data integrity issues between front and back while dealing with a poor REST framework called Tastypie, which were my most interesting and challenging responsibilities in Vaihtorinki.
